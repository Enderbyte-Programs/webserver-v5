The Downfall of Napoleon

  • In 1808, a rebel force in Spain was organized by Spanish revolutionaries and backed by Britain, France's sworn enemy.
  • They fought France using Guerilla tactics, which are very sneaky. The French army was not prepared.
  • This caused France to lose some territory in Spain.

The Continental System

Napoleon devised an economic system to be rolled out to all of France's puppet states. It was called the Continental System. It decreed:

  1. French Subjects may not trade with Britain
  2. French Subjects may only purchase goods from fellow French Subjects
  3. Ships found trading with Britain shall be severely punished

Areas affected by the Continental System

The Continental System was designed to cripple Britain's economy.

  • It did not have the intended effect; smuggling became a common way to trade.
  • It also sowed discontent among conquered subjects, who began plotting another war.

Defeat

  • Napoleon made his most ambitious move yet in 1812. He invaded Russia.
  • He got all the way to Moscow, only to find the city burned.
  • He decided to turn back, but was caught in a horrific winter.
  • 500,000 out of the 600,000 men Napoleon sent were killed.
  • Napoleon and his army suffered further losses in Leipzig (now part of Germany).
  • Napoleon Bonaparte was so embarrassed by his defeat, that he abdicated.
  • He was exiled by his enemies to St. Elba, no longer an emperor.
    • St. Elba is a small, impoverished island off the coast of Italy.
  • King Louis 18 was instated as King and promptly repealed revolutionary ideals. The French Revolution had come full circle.

Life in Exile

  • Napoleon was horrified with his situation. He tried to commit suicide by poison, but the poison failed and he only became extremely ill.
  • Napoleon remained depressed on his tiny island exile.
  • However, not a year after his exile, he plotted escape.
  • In 1818, He stole a ship while his British supervisor was off on a trip and sailed back to France.
  • When the Coalition puppet government heard of this, an army was sent to stop Napoleon's approach.
  • However, when Napoleon first encountered the army, they immediately defected to his side.
    • A prime reason for this was that the people were becoming angry about the return of the negligent monarchy.
    • Though Napoleon forced them into war, he was still better than suffering under feudalism.
  • Napoleon and his reformed army retook Paris. The Emperor was back.
