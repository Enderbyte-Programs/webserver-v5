Napoleon's Propaganda

  • Napoleon was a skilled propagandist who could easily manipulate the emotions of the people.
  • During and after his reign, he commissioned many paintings which glorified him and his actions, making him seem like unto a god in the eyes of the people.
  • One such painting was Napoleon In His Study, which propagandized to the people what a hard worker The Emperor was.

Napoleon In His Study
  • When Napoleon Bonaparte invaded other nations, he portrayed himself as a man of peace, liberating them from their cruel oppressors.
  • However, the conquered populace soon discovered that they were just swapping one tyrannical government for another. Napoleon was definitely not a man of peace.
  • Even nearing his death far away from his former empire, Napoleon continued to write glorious memoirs and autobiographies.
  • He truly ensured that he would never be forgotten.

Napoleon and the Slaves

  • Napoleon aided slaves organizing a revolt against the British.
  • He told the revolution leader Toussaint that he understood the struggle of the Haitian people.
    • This was partially true, as he had experienced some oppression as a schoolboy.
    • However, it was mostly propaganda to get the Haitians to accept French martial law.
  • However, Napoleon turned out to be tyrannical on this count too.
    • The Haitians established their own government on constitution.
    • Dictator Napoleon did not like this at all, and he sent soldiers to Hispaniola (The island where Haiti is located).
    • The country's leaders were imprisoned and Haiti became another French territory.
